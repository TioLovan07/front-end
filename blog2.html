Brew Coffee Using Special Coffee Filter

Juna / 15 December 2023

Coffee paper is a special filter for brewing pour-over coffee made from paper. Based on the production process, there are white (bleached) filter paper and brown (natural) filter paper. Coffee paper also comes in various shapes depending on the type of pour-over coffee equipment used and its brewing capacity, V60 coffee filter paper, Kalita Wave filter paper, and Flat Bottom filter paper.

To serve coffee using filter paper, it's a good idea to first understand the differences in coffee paper to be used, the relationship between coffee grind level, coffee powder ratio, and hot water, which also plays an important role in manual brewing of pour-over coffee.

It is believed that White (Bleached) Coffee Filter Paper is better for filtering pour-over coffee extraction because the taste of the paper is not as pronounced, whereas Natural/Unbleached Brown Filter Coffee Paper sometimes produces the taste of the paper when extracting pour-over coffee. In the process on a scale that is still safe, natural filter coffee paper is more environmentally friendly than white filter coffee paper which can pollute the environment. However, it all depends on the taste of the coffee lover when it comes to brewing pour-over coffee.

Rinsing the coffee paper before starting the brewing process really contributes to creating a delicious pour-over coffee taste. Coffee paper is actually made from paper, at a detailed level if not mentioned, the taste of paper or other negative flavors caused by paper will be transferred to your coffee. The reason is, apart from the cleanliness factor, rinsing the coffee paper also opens the pores of the coffee paper so that the coffee extraction process is easier when it meets the brewing water.
